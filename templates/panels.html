The Panels

If we look at the sympathetic intelligence construct, which can be found on the concept side of the website, you will see that sympathetic intelligence is ubiquitous in our lives and is present within, between and amongst us. The last tier defines society in its entirety, for we live, we learn and we work.

In every context where we interact, sympathetic intelligence is present. In the last layer of the third tier work there are three vital services in society they are:- healthcare education and policing. The reason these three professions are so important is because the quality of our social existence depends on them. These are essential services, the quality of which defines the quality of each society. That's not to say that we don't need barbers, that we don't need engineers or mechanics - we do! But the impact of these three primary professions on the quality of our society is critical. To an extent these professions even mirror the layers within the third tier as follows; Health is critical to our lives and even the ability to live; Education is critical to our capacity to learn and; Policing in our society involves order, process, rules and regulations - so that we can work together effectively.

The mission of the center is to bring depth to life. That's because the more depth we have in the way we engage with each other, the better our lives. To achieve this, we need sympathetic intelligence. When our engagement is in sympathy with the needs of any moment, then the fullest depth of that momentâ€™s needs, are met.

To deliver on our mission, we believe research in these three key areas, primarily at the outset is the right way to begin. Once we can identify existing sympathetic intelligence, understand it better and develop ways to improve and optimize sympathetic intelligence in our health care, education and policing we can really achieve our goal of bringing depth to life. And so, the first step in putting this mission in action is the setting up of these three panels.
